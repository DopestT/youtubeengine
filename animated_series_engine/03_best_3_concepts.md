# 03 — Best 3 Concepts

Scored across the 8 criteria you asked for. Each criterion is 1–10; the table at the end
totals them. The three winners aren't just the funniest — they're the ones that are
**cheapest to produce consistently** *and* **easiest to turn into a recognizable brand**,
which is the whole point of building an IP engine.

The winners: **#9 Sprout**, **#2 Tiny Apex**, **#6 Ghosted**.

---

## 🥇 #9 — Sprout

> A tiny, overconfident houseplant is convinced it's destined to rule the world, but it
> can't move, so it must manipulate the oblivious humans around it.

**Why it wins:**
- **Simplicity (10):** One set (a windowsill/room), one mostly-static lead. The comedy is
  carried by voiceover + tiny reactions. This is the single most producible concept here.
- **Repeatability (9):** Infinite "evil plan #N" format episodes that all reset to the
  pot by the end.
- **Visual identity (9):** A leafy villain with a tiny face and an ownable green is an
  instantly recognizable silhouette.
- **Joke potential (8):** Deadpan villain monologue + the gap between ambition and being
  a plant = reliable subversion engine.
- **Emotional hook (7):** Underdog/lonely-dreamer undercurrent; people root for the tiny
  guy who'll never win.
- **Monetization (8):** Desk plush, sticker, "world domination" mug, pot/planter merch —
  obvious and cheap to make.
- **Ease of AI production (10):** Static framing, minimal motion, talking-head friendly —
  ideal for current AI animation tools.
- **Brandability (8):** Strong name, strong catchphrase potential ("Soon… so very soon."),
  ownable color and shape.

**Best as:** your **fastest-to-launch** pilot. If you need to be posting this week with
the least effort, start here.

---

## 🥈 #2 — Tiny Apex

> A house cat is genuinely convinced it's an elite apex predator. Reality disagrees,
> constantly.

**Why it wins:**
- **Simplicity (8):** Familiar premise, no world-building tax — everyone instantly gets a
  cat with delusions of grandeur.
- **Repeatability (9):** "Biscuit attempts to hunt [ordinary object]" is an endless format.
- **Visual identity (8):** A chubby cat with intense predator-eyes is a meme-ready design.
- **Joke potential (9):** Self-belief vs. reality is a bottomless well; physical comedy
  travels across every language.
- **Emotional hook (8):** We love a confident little failure; secretly it just wants to
  matter.
- **Monetization (10):** Pets = the strongest merch and collab category on the internet.
  Plush, apparel, pet-brand integrations.
- **Ease of AI production (8):** More motion than Sprout, but cats are well-represented in
  AI models and the gags are short.
- **Brandability (9):** Animal mascots are the most license-able, broadest-appeal IP type.

**Best as:** your **highest-ceiling** pilot. Biggest potential reach and merch revenue if
it hits.

---

## 🥉 #6 — Ghosted

> A recently-dead rookie ghost is terrible at haunting and has to file performance reviews
> with a bored afterlife HR department.

**Why it wins:**
- **Simplicity (8):** Spooky-cute is a clear, established lane; a sheet-ghost is the
  simplest possible character to draw and animate.
- **Repeatability (8):** "Sheet tries to scare [person/thing]" + "HR review" are two
  reliable formats.
- **Visual identity (9):** The sheet silhouette is iconic, minimal, and instantly ownable.
- **Joke potential (8):** Failure-to-scare + corporate-afterlife satire = two joke
  engines.
- **Emotional hook (8):** Wanting to belong / be taken seriously after death is
  surprisingly poignant.
- **Monetization (9):** Plush, seasonal (October) spikes, sticker/apparel — spooky-cute
  merch sells extremely well.
- **Ease of AI production (8):** A floating white shape is forgiving for AI animation
  consistency.
- **Brandability (9):** Minimal, memeable, seasonal evergreen — easy to make a logo and a
  comfort-character out of.

**Best as:** your **most brandable / merch-first** pilot, with built-in seasonal surges.

## Recommendation

Pilot **all three** — they're cheap enough that you can. But sequence them:

1. **Launch Sprout first** (week 1) — lowest production cost, fastest to a posting rhythm,
   lets you debug the pipeline on easy mode.
2. **Launch Tiny Apex second** (week 2–3) — highest commercial ceiling; test whether you
   can hit broad reach.
3. **Hold Ghosted** as the merch/seasonal play; ramp it toward Q3 for an October surge.

Then let the scorecard in `05` decide which one gets the bulk of your production capacity.
