# 02 — Concept Development Matrix

Ten original animated series concepts generated by the engine in `01`. None are based on
or derivative of any existing IP. Each is concept-agnostic about products — pick by
performance, not by attachment.

**Scoring:** Production Difficulty (1 = trivial to make with AI tools, 10 = very hard) ·
Monetization Potential (1 = weak, 10 = strong IP/merch upside).

---

### 1. Office of Slightly Wrong Magic
- **Premise:** A junior wizard works the complaints desk at a magic bureau where every
  spell is technically correct but practically a disaster.
- **Main characters:** Pell (the Innocent intern who takes rules literally), Marg (the
  Burnout senior clerk), Director Vex (the Authority who only cares about paperwork).
- **Recurring conflict:** Character vs. The Rules of the World — the magic always does
  exactly what's asked, never what's meant.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** instant visual gags, self-contained "be careful what you
  wish for" loop, satisfying turn every time.
- **Why it could grow into IP:** rich world, infinite spell premises, very merchable
  mascot, easy to expand to longer "cases."
- **Production difficulty:** 5 · **Monetization:** 8

---

### 2. Tiny Apex
- **Premise:** A house cat is genuinely convinced it's an elite apex predator; reality
  disagrees, constantly.
- **Main characters:** Biscuit (the True Believer cat), the Dog (the Straight Man who's
  unbothered), the Laser Dot (the Wildcard nemesis it can never catch).
- **Recurring conflict:** Want vs. Flaw — wants to be feared, is adored instead.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** animals = universal reach, no language barrier, ultra
  shareable, slapstick travels globally.
- **Why it could grow into IP:** maximum merch (plush, stickers), catchphrase-ready,
  toddler-to-adult appeal, easy crossover collabs.
- **Production difficulty:** 4 · **Monetization:** 10

---

### 3. The Last Honest Goblin
- **Premise:** In a fantasy economy run on lies and scams, one goblin is cursed to only
  tell the truth — which somehow ruins everyone's day.
- **Main characters:** Nub (the lead, painfully honest), Sly (the Rival con-goblin), Mer
  (the Anchor who roots for him).
- **Recurring conflict:** Lead vs. Rival + Want vs. Flaw — wants to fit in, can't lie.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** the truth-bomb turn is a perfect punchline machine.
- **Why it could grow into IP:** strong moral core, quotable, edgy-but-kind tone, good
  for a comic/zine.
- **Production difficulty:** 5 · **Monetization:** 7

---

### 4. Customer Support for the Apocalypse
- **Premise:** A chipper call-center agent provides tech support for people surviving the
  end of the world, reading from a script that never fits the emergency.
- **Main characters:** Dot-Matrix "Dot" (the relentlessly upbeat agent), Hank (the
  panicking caller archetype, rotating), the Supervisor (Authority, never available).
- **Recurring conflict:** Character vs. Authority / corporate-script absurdity.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** punchy two-hander format, audio-driven, cheap to animate
  (mostly one agent + a screen).
- **Why it could grow into IP:** infinitely topical, satirical, strong adult skew, great
  for collabs and brand parody integrations.
- **Production difficulty:** 3 · **Monetization:** 6

---

### 5. Brunchosaurs
- **Premise:** A group of dinosaurs runs a struggling weekend brunch spot, blissfully
  unaware they're dinosaurs in a human city.
- **Main characters:** Rex (the Striver owner with big dreams), Tris (the Enabler), Ankle
  (the Burnout line cook), Karen-the-comet (the looming Wildcard threat).
- **Recurring conflict:** Lead vs. The World (a city not built for dinosaurs) +
  small-business chaos.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** absurd visual premise, food content overlaps a huge niche,
  warm-absurd tone = broad reach.
- **Why it could grow into IP:** mascots galore, food/merch crossover, cozy fandom.
- **Production difficulty:** 6 · **Monetization:** 8

---

### 6. Ghosted
- **Premise:** A recently-dead ghost is terrible at haunting and has to file performance
  reviews with a bored afterlife HR department.
- **Main characters:** Sheet (the well-meaning rookie ghost), Mort (the Authority HR
  reaper), a Human who refuses to be scared (the Straight Man).
- **Recurring conflict:** Want vs. Flaw — wants to be scary, is endearing.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** spooky-cute is evergreen + huge seasonal spikes (Oct).
- **Why it could grow into IP:** very merchable (the sheet design), wholesome-creepy
  niche, plush/sticker gold.
- **Production difficulty:** 4 · **Monetization:** 9

---

### 7. Two Stars
- **Premise:** A delusional food critic and his long-suffering stomach (a talking
  character) review increasingly questionable meals around the city.
- **Main characters:** Reginald (the Striver critic, pure ego), Gut (the Straight Man,
  literally his stomach), the Chef (rotating Rival).
- **Recurring conflict:** Want vs. Flaw — wants prestige, has no taste.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** food + reaction format, two-character banter, low scene
  complexity.
- **Why it could grow into IP:** quotable ratings catchphrase, food-brand collabs, easy
  guest cameos.
- **Production difficulty:** 4 · **Monetization:** 7

---

### 8. The Intern of Olympus
- **Premise:** A mortal intern is hired by petty, dramatic gods and has to manage their
  god-sized tantrums with a clipboard and zero authority.
- **Main characters:** Effie (the competent mortal intern, Straight Man), Zev (the
  blustering boss-god), Nyx (the chaotic coworker-god, Wildcard).
- **Recurring conflict:** Character vs. Authority — mortal vs. divine egos.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** mythology is recognizable shorthand, big personalities,
  endless "boss from hell" relatability.
- **Why it could grow into IP:** strong cast, expandable pantheon, workplace-comedy bones
  for longer episodes.
- **Production difficulty:** 6 · **Monetization:** 7

---

### 9. Sprout
- **Premise:** A tiny, overconfident houseplant believes it's destined to take over the
  world but cannot move, so it has to manipulate the humans around it.
- **Main characters:** Sprout (the Schemer plant), the Oblivious Owner, the Cat (the
  Rival who sees through it).
- **Recurring conflict:** Schemer's plan vs. the obvious flaw (it's a plant).
- **Why it works for Shorts:** single static set, ultra-cheap to animate, deadpan
  voiceover carries it.
- **Why it could grow into IP:** adorable villain mascot, desk-merch/plush, strong
  catchphrase potential.
- **Production difficulty:** 2 · **Monetization:** 8

---

### 10. Night Shift Yeti
- **Premise:** A gentle yeti works the overnight shift at a 24-hour convenience store in a
  weird little town, dealing with strange customers and small loneliness.
- **Main characters:** Bom (the Heart-forward yeti), the Regulars (rotating oddballs), the
  Raccoon (the Enabler sidekick living in the dumpster).
- **Recurring conflict:** Order vs. Chaos + gentle emotional beats.
- **Why it works for Shorts:** cozy-core is a massive trend, low scene count, warm tone =
  high loyalty and saves.
- **Why it could grow into IP:** deep emotional well for longer episodes, lo-fi/merch
  crossover, strong "comfort character."
- **Production difficulty:** 5 · **Monetization:** 7
